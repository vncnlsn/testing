About APS Land

Our History

The company started because someone believed this work deserved to be done honestly.

Allstate Permit Services was founded in the late 1990s out of a straightforward conviction: the landmen running permit acquisition either cared too little about the people they were dealing with, or didn't understand the technical side well enough to do the job right. There was a gap, and someone was going to fill it.

We spent the first decade building relationships — with survey crews, with county clerks, with the landowners who remembered us from the last project and called us directly for the next one. That's still how most of our work comes in.

The rebrand to APS Land reflects what the company has always actually done: not just permits, but the full range of land work that gets energy and infrastructure projects off the drawing board and into the ground. The name caught up to the reality.

What hasn't changed since day one

  • We don't fabricate title research to speed up a timeline. If the chain of ownership has a gap, we find it and document it — before it becomes a field problem.
  • Our agents are vetted and experienced. We don't put someone on a project who hasn't earned the trust of the people they're about to knock on doors for.
  • We track every dollar of permit cost and report it accurately. No surprises at the end of a project.
  • When something goes wrong — a fence gets clipped, a rut gets left — we already know about it before the landowner calls. Because our people were there.
How We Work

Three things that don't change regardless of project size, client, or deadline pressure.

Accurate records, not optimistic ones.

Title research is only useful if it reflects what's actually in the courthouse. We build complete records, including the problem parcels, and deliver documentation that holds up in disputes — not just documentation that allows a project to proceed on schedule.

Agent quality over agent count.

We staff projects with experienced contract agents who have worked with us across multiple projects and know how to represent a client without creating liability. The size of our roster is less important than the judgment of the people on it.

Agreements that hold when tested.

A signed permit or access agreement is only as good as the conversation that produced it. We document commitments, notify landowners before crews arrive, and follow through on every condition in every agreement — because the next project in that county depends on it.

For Landowners

We're the ones who work for a fair deal — not just a signed one.

When an energy company needs access to your land, they hire a land company to come talk to you. APS Land is sometimes that company. And while our client is writing the check, our job — practically and legally — is to produce agreements that are accurate and fair, not just agreements.

That means we tell you exactly what survey crews are authorized to do and what they are not. We put compensation terms in writing before anyone shows up. We give you a contact name and phone number, and we answer when you call.

If something goes wrong — crops affected, a gate left open, equipment on a part of your land that wasn't in the agreement — you tell us, and we handle it. Not the crew foreman. Not a project manager in Houston. Us.
