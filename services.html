What We Do

Permit acquisition, land services,
and everything in between.

APS has handled the full range of land and permitting work for energy and infrastructure projects since 1980. Here is exactly what that looks like.

01

Permit Acquisition

APS has secured permits for seismic surveys, pipeline construction, and infrastructure projects across 27+ states. We handle direct landowner contact, negotiation, documentation, and permit execution — with a clean paper trail from the first conversation to the signed document.

Our experience spans surface use agreements, seismic entry permits, pipeline right-of-way, and state agency coordination. Every permit is handled with the same professional care we would want if it were our own land.

  • Seismic and geophysical survey permits
  • Pipeline and utility corridor permits
  • State and federal agency coordination
  • Permit documentation and filing
  • Landowner contact and follow-up

02

Title & Land Research

Before any project breaks ground, the land underneath it has to be fully understood. APS traces courthouse records, verifies current ownership, maps parcel boundaries, and flags mineral rights conflicts before they become project problems.

  • Surface and mineral title research
  • Courthouse and county record review
  • Ownership chain verification
  • Parcel mapping and boundary confirmation
  • Mineral rights tracing and conflict identification

03

Right-of-Way Acquisition

Negotiating right-of-way requires equal parts patience, precision, and respect for the landowner across the table. APS negotiates, documents, and executes right-of-way agreements for pipelines, power lines, and surface use — treating every landowner interaction as a long-term relationship, not a transaction.

  • Pipeline and utility ROW negotiation
  • Surface use agreement execution
  • Landowner relations management
  • Damage claims and compensation handling
  • ROW documentation and recording

Field Oversight & Closeout

A permit is only as good as what happens in the field. APS provides on-site oversight during active operations to ensure that crews are following permit terms, access routes are respected, and landowner agreements are honored from first day to final reclamation.

  • On-site crew and operations oversight
  • Access route and permit compliance monitoring
  • Damage identification and documentation
  • Landowner damage claims management
  • Restoration verification and final closeout
